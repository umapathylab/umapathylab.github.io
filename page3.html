Biodiversity Conservation & Monitoring Lab

Dr. G. Umapathy's Group

Our Approach

  • Conservation Breeding
  • Non-invasive Endocrine Monitoring
  • Conservation Genetics
  • Genomics

We use artificial insemination and captive breeding to revive populations of endangered species which are at the brink of extinction.

We are specialized in the detection of steroid hormones in the fecal samples for non-invasive monitoring of reproductive status and stress levels in many species.

We use polymorphic DNA markers and phylogenetics to establish the population structures, relatedness, and monitor the genetic diversity & gene flow in the wild.

We use Next-generation sequencing and Bioinformatics to construct new reference genomes, to monitor community biodiversity, and to answer intriguing evolutionary questions.
